Is it safe? Can I undo a clean?

Yes. MacCleanUp moves items to the Trash rather than deleting them, so you can restore anything from the Trash. You also review and select every item before cleaning, and protected locations (System, Photos, Mail, Messages, iCloud, live data) are never touched.

Why does it ask for folder access?

MacCleanUp is sandboxed, so it can only scan folders you grant it. Granting your Home folder once lets it check the common places clutter hides (Library caches, Downloads, Documents, Movies). You can add or revoke folders anytime via the gear icon → Settings.

Why is the first scan an estimate?

To stay fast, the opening scan takes a quick look and gives you a sense of what's reclaimable (shown with a “~”). When you run an actual cleanup, MacCleanUp does the full, exact count before anything is moved.

What does each cleanup do?

  • Free Up Space Fast — the largest files, ready to clear immediately.
  • Clear System Junk — caches, logs and temporary files.
  • Deep Clean — junk, large files and duplicates together.

Does it collect any data?

No. The app runs entirely on your Mac, makes no network connections, and collects nothing. See our Privacy Policy.

System requirements

macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later.
